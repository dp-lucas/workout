## Key Takeaways

### 1. Start with your personal goal
- No perfect universal plan — train for what *you* enjoy and what *you* want to accomplish
- If you love running more, run more. If you love lifting more, lift more. But find ways to incorporate both.
- Identify a specific goal (endurance event, body comp, strength) and let that drive programming

### 2. Training frequency — minimum 3+3
- **3x/week strength training**
- **3x/week cardio** (zone 2–3, low intensity)
- Cardio doesn't have to be running — cycling, swimming, walking all count
- This is the baseline for health span and lifespan benefits

### 3. Progressive overload — the #1 growth principle
- Same stimulus over time = adaptation plateau, not growth
- Levers to manipulate: **exercise selection, reps, sets, weight, rest periods**
- **Document everything** — miles, paces, HR, exercises, reps, sets, weights, RPE
- Running-specific rule: don't increase weekly mileage by more than **10% per week**

### 4. Strength vs. hypertrophy — know the difference
- **Hypertrophy (size):** mechanical tension, isolating muscle fibers, maximizing recruitment
- **Strength (force production):** neuromuscular, skill-based, full-body coordination
- Bigger muscle ≠ stronger muscle (bodybuilders vs. powerlifters)
- Practicing a movement pattern (skill) improves strength even without more muscle
- Both matter for a hybrid athlete, but they require different training approaches
