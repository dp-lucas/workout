WORKOUT

Hybrid Athlete — Built to Lift & Run

DAY A: Quads + Vertical Push/Pull

Focus: Quads (squat + single-leg), Vertical Push, Vertical Pull · Calves

6 exercises 19 working sets ~60 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position (hips + thoracic for dips and chin-ups) — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (hip rotation for squat and split squat) — Ankle Rockers: 2 × 8/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (ankle dorsiflexion for slant board squat depth and calf work) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for dips and chin-ups) + Bodyweight Squats: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-0-1-0 (quad activation before barbell squat) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core
Hanging Leg Raises
2 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60s

Dead hang, posterior pelvic tilt first, then raise legs. Control the descent — no swinging. Straight legs if possible, bent knees to scale down. The hang itself decompresses the spine. Hip flexor strength directly powers your running knee drive. 2s lowering legs | 1s raising legs | 1s hold at top.

02
Quads
Slant Board Barbell Squat
3 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Set feet on the slant board (heels on the elevated surface), about shoulder-width, toes out slightly. Bar on upper back, brace hard — ribs down, full breath. Break at hips and knees together, sit between your hips. Hit at least parallel; depth you own with a neutral spine. Drive up through mid-foot — knees track over toes, no valgus collapse. Same weight across all 3 sets — set 3 should be a grind. The wedge frees the ankle so you stay upright and load the quads without turning it into a good morning. 3s lowering | 1s pause in the hole | 1s up | no pause at top.

03
Single-Leg Quads
Bulgarian Split Squat
3 × 8–10/legTempo: 2-1-1-0Rest: 90s after each leg

Rear foot on bench, laces down. Front foot far enough forward that shin is roughly vertical at the bottom. Torso can lean slightly forward — keep front heel planted. Lower until rear knee hovers just above the floor — control the eccentric, no crashing. Press through the whole front foot; finish with hip and knee extended. Do all reps for one leg, rest, then the other — 3 sets per leg. Matches single-leg stability and knee tracking you use on every running stride. If Tuesday's easy run feels brutal, drop to 2 sets per leg before you ditch the run. 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s up | no long pause at top.

04
Vertical Push
Weighted Dips (Lean Forward)
3 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Lean forward ~30° to shift emphasis to chest and front delts. Shoulders down and back, elbows track slightly outward. Lower until upper arm is parallel to floor, drive up. Dips are the upper body squat — chest, triceps, and front delts in one compound movement. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pressing up | no pause at top.

05
Vertical Pull
Weighted Chin-Ups
4 × 6–8Tempo: 2-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Drive elbows down to your pockets. No swinging or kipping. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10–20s) — isometric peak contraction for lat density. Builds the lats and upper back that keep you upright during long runs. 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | explosive pull up | 1s hold at top.

06
Calves
Standing Calf Raises
4 × 8–10Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Full stretch at the bottom, full squeeze at the top — pause 2s at both ends. Heavy weight, strict form, no bouncing. Standing position loads the gastrocnemius — Achilles tendon loading, the #1 injury prevention exercise for runners. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

DAY B: Arms + Shoulders + Prehab

Focus: Biceps, Triceps, Shoulder Press, Lateral Raises, Face Pulls · Copenhagen Prehab

6 exercises 18 working sets ~45 min total
WARM-UP (~3 min): Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for pressing and face pulls) + Band Dislocates: 1 × 10, Tempo: slow and controlled (shoulder mobility for overhead press) (Rest: no rest, flow between drills)
01
Hip Stability (Frontal Plane)
Copenhagen Side Planks
2 × 20–30s/sideTempo: Isometric HoldRest: 60s

Lie on your side, top leg on a bench with inner thigh resting on the edge. Lift your hips and hold — bottom leg hangs or tucks. Loads the hip adductors isometrically in the frontal plane — directly prevents groin strains and knee valgus during running. Scale: bent knee on bench (easier) → straight leg (harder) → bottom leg raised (hardest). Keep hips stacked, don't rotate. Zero leg fatigue — isometric hold doesn't create soreness that affects Thursday's fartlek.

02
Vertical Press
Overhead Press
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 2m

Barbell or dumbbells — pick whichever you can progress. Brace hard, ribs down. Press straight overhead, lock out with biceps near ears. Don't lean back — if you have to arch your lower back, the weight is too heavy. Completes the pressing pattern: Day A has dips (vertical, chest-emphasis), Day C has incline bench (horizontal), Day B has overhead press (vertical, delt-emphasis). 2s lowering | 1s pressing up | 1s hold at lockout.

03
Rear Delts / Posture
Face Pulls
3 × 12–15Tempo: 2-1-1-2Rest: 60s

Pull towards your eyes. Rotate hands back at the end (double biceps pose). Trains rear delts and external rotators that counterbalance forward-shoulder posture from running and pressing. Non-negotiable for shoulder health — balances all the pressing across the week. 2s letting rope return | 1s pause | 1s pulling to face | 2s squeeze in external rotation.

04
Side Delts
Lateral Raises
4 × 12–15Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60s

Slight forward lean, lead with elbows, raise to just above shoulder height. All weekly side delt volume consolidated here — 4 focused sets instead of 3+3 spread across two days. Side delts only grow from direct isolation; no compound covers them. Light weight, strict form, chase the burn. 2s lowering | 1s raising | 1s hold at top.

05
Biceps
Barbell Curls
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 90s

Strict form — elbows pinned to your sides, no swinging. Full extension at the bottom, squeeze at the top. Chin-ups on Day A hit biceps hard as a secondary mover; this is your direct bicep work to fill the gap. If the barbell bothers your wrists, use an EZ-bar or dumbbells. 2s lowering | 1s curling up | 1s squeeze at top.

06
Triceps
Overhead Tricep Extension
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 90s

Cable or dumbbell — overhead position stretches the long head of the tricep under load, which dips alone don't fully hit. Keep elbows pointed forward, lower behind your head, extend to full lockout. Dips on Day A hit triceps as a secondary mover; this isolates them through full range. 2s lowering | 1s extending up | 1s squeeze at lockout.

DAY C: Hams/Glutes + Horizontal Push/Pull

Focus: Hamstrings, Glutes, Horizontal Push, Horizontal Pull · Core + Calves

6 exercises 18 working sets ~60 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 — Cat-Cow: 2 × 8, Tempo: 3-0-3-0 (spinal segmentation for the hinge pattern) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder health for bench and rows) + Glute Bridges: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-1-1-2 (glute and hamstring activation for RDLs and hip thrusts) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core (Anti-Rotation)
Pallof Press
2 × 10/sideTempo: 1-2-1-0Rest: 60s

Cable at chest height, stand perpendicular to the machine. Press the handle straight out from your chest and hold — resist the rotation. The further you extend your arms, the harder it gets. Keep hips and shoulders square, brace your whole trunk. Trains the anti-rotation your core needs every running stride — your trunk resists rotation thousands of times per run. 1s pressing out | 2s hold at full extension | 1s returning to chest.

02
Posterior Chain
Barbell Romanian Deadlift
3 × 5Tempo: 3-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Hinge at hips, push them back. Bar drags along your thighs — keep it in contact the whole way down. Slight knee bend, feel the deep hamstring stretch at the bottom — drive hips forward to lockout. Same weight all 3 sets — heavy but controlled. Strong hamstrings and glutes are your running injury insurance and power your stride. 3s lowering | 1s pause at stretch | explosive drive | 1s lockout.

03
Glutes
Barbell Hip Thrusts
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-0-1-2Rest: 2m

Upper back on bench, feet flat, shin vertical at the top. Drive through heels, squeeze glutes hard at lockout — hold 2s. Don't hyperextend the lower back — think "ribs down, belt buckle to chin." The #1 glute builder and your running injury insurance. Protects knees, powers uphill running. 2s lowering hips | 1s driving up | 2s squeeze at lockout.

04
Horizontal Push
Incline Bench Press
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-1-1-0Rest: 2m

Bar to upper chest (below collarbone). Control the descent strictly. Same weight all 3 sets — grind it out. Pairs with forward-lean dips on Day A for complete chest coverage across the week. 2s lowering bar to chest | 1s pause at chest | 1s pressing up | no pause at lockout.

05
Horizontal Pull
Seated Cable Rows
4 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 2m

Sit tall, chest up, feet braced. Pull handle to lower ribcage, squeeze shoulder blades together. Don't lean back — torso stays upright throughout. Chin-ups hit lats (vertical pull on Day A), rows hit rhomboids and mid/lower traps (horizontal pull). Your mid-back keeps you upright on long runs — when it fatigues you hunch forward and breathing degrades. 2s releasing cable forward | 1s pulling to ribcage | 1s squeeze at top.

06
Calves
Seated Calf Raises
3 × 10–15Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Knee bent at 90° loads the soleus — the deeper, wider calf muscle underneath the gastrocnemius. The soleus is the primary calf muscle used at endurance pace, making this the more running-specific variation. Day A standing hits gastroc, Day C seated hits soleus — full calf coverage. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

RUN DAYS: Tue · Thu · Sun

Easy Run · Fartlek · Long Run — 80/20 Polarized

3 runs/week 80/20 polarized ~95–115 min/week
WARM-UP (every run): Ankle Circles: 10/side — Single Leg Calf Raises: 10/side (slow & controlled) — Wall Tibialis Raises: 15-25 reps (back against wall, pull toes up, lower slowly — shin splint prevention) — Elephant Walk: 10/side — Leg Swings Front-to-Back: 10/side — Leg Swings Side-to-Side: 10/side
TUE
Recovery Run (Post-Squat Day)
Easy Run
25–30 minRPE 3 · Zone 2HR: 120–150 bpm

Post-quad day — legs will be sore and that's normal. Walk the first 5-10 minutes if stiff. Genuinely easy: you should hold a full conversation without gasping. If HR drifts above 150, slow down or walk. This is the shortest, easiest run of the week. Walk breaks are always OK. The goal is blood flow and aerobic base, not performance. Easy running post-legs actually speeds recovery by increasing circulation.

THU
Quality Day (Freshest Legs)
Fartlek
30–35 min totalPickups: RPE 6-7Recovery: RPE 3

Freshest running legs of the week — 72 hours post-Day A, Day B yesterday was arms-only (zero leg fatigue). Structure: 10 min easy warm-up jog → 8 × (45s pickup / 90s easy jog) → 10 min easy cooldown. Pickups are "comfortably faster" — not sprinting, not racing. Go by feel, not pace. If legs feel flat or sore, skip the pickups and make it another easy run — the fartlek is earned, not mandatory. Total hard running is only ~6 minutes out of the whole session.

SUN
Endurance Day (Post-Rest)
Long Run
40–50 minRPE 3-4 · Zone 2Easiest pace of the week

Fresh legs — full rest day Saturday, 48 hours post-Day C. This is the longest run of the week: time on feet matters, speed doesn't. Run slower than you think you should. Walk breaks are encouraged, not a failure. Build duration gradually — add 5 minutes every 2 weeks. No time pressure, open schedule. Post-run: 5 min walk cooldown, then calf stretch + hip flexor stretch (30s each side).

Then static stretches: Standing Quad Stretch 30s/side — Standing Calf Stretch (straight leg for gastroc, bent knee for soleus) 30s each/side — Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch 30s/side — Hamstring stretch or standing toe touch 30s. Running Rules

1.

Talk Test Is King: If you can't hold a conversation, slow down or walk. HR target 120-150 bpm on easy days. RPE is primary, HR is confirmation.

2.

80/20 Polarized: 2 easy runs (Tue + Sun) + 1 quality session (Thu). Never turn an easy day into a hard day — that's the #1 beginner mistake.

3.

Fartlek Is Earned: Thursday pickups are by feel, not pace. If legs feel flat or sore, skip pickups and run easy. Consistency beats intensity.

4.

Pain Rule: Sharp or localized pain (shin, Achilles, knee) = drop to 2 runs that week. Cut Tuesday, keep Thursday easy + Sunday.

5.

10% Rule: Never increase total weekly running time by more than 10% per week. Tendons and bones adapt slower than lungs.

6.

Form Cues: Land under your hips, not out front. Run quietly — loud footfalls mean overstriding. Relax shoulders, hands loose. Eyes forward 20-30m ahead.

Overtraining Warning Signs

⚠

Physical: Resting HR elevated 7-10+ bpm above baseline. Same pace feels harder than usual (RPE creep). Persistent heavy/sore muscles beyond normal DOMS (>72 hrs). Recurring colds or upper respiratory infections. Unexplained weight loss.

⚠

Mental: Loss of motivation to train. Mood disturbances — irritability, anxiety, feeling flat. Poor sleep despite fatigue. Loss of appetite.

⚠

Action: If 2+ signs persist for more than 1 week, take a full rest week — no lifting, no running. Sleep, eat well, walk if you want. Concurrent training (lifting + running) compounds recovery demands. Catching it early prevents weeks of forced rest later.

Cooldown (Every Lifting Session)

Dead Hangs2–3 × 30–45sRest: 60s

Decompress the spine after every session. Relax everything — shoulders, lats, hips. Grip work is a bonus.

Block 1 — Lifting Protocol (Weeks 1–4)

1.

Weeks 1–3 — Progressive Overload: When you hit the top of the rep range on ALL sets of an exercise, add weight next session (2.5 kg upper, 5 kg lower). If you miss reps, stay at the same weight.

2.

Week 4 — Deload: Same exercises, same weight. Cut sets in half on all 3 lift days — 4→2, 3→2, 2→1. Keep intensity, slash volume. You will come back stronger. Run deload matches (see Run tab).

3.

Neither Owns the Other: Lifting and running are equal priorities. If a lift makes you too sore to run the next day, reduce lifting weight by 10%. If a run leaves you too fatigued to lift, shorten the run. Both must coexist — adjust whichever is out of balance. With 6 training days and 1 rest day, recovery margin is thin — sleep and nutrition are non-negotiable.

The Hybrid Athlete Rules

1.

Not a Bodybuilder Who Runs. Not a Runner Who Lifts: You are a hybrid athlete — the best of both worlds working in sync. Lifting builds the dense, balanced, athletic physique. Running builds the cardiovascular engine, VO2Max, and keeps you lean. Neither is subordinate. Both serve the whole.

2.

The Goal: A good-looking, balanced, healthy, functional, flexible, toned, dense and well-built body that can comfortably run 10-15K with strong lungs and a powerful cardiovascular system. Not a marathon. Not a bodybuilding stage. The athletic hybrid.

3.

Compounds + Targeted Isolation: Slant Board Squat, Bulgarian Split Squat, Dips, Chin-Ups, RDL, Hip Thrusts, Incline Bench, Cable Rows. Vertical + horizontal push and pull covered. Leg Curls for hamstring isolation on Day B. Day B handles all arm, shoulder, and prehab work — OHP, Face Pulls, Lateral Raises, Copenhagens, Curls, Tricep Extensions — keeping A and C short and focused.

4.

80/20 Running: 2 easy runs + 1 quality session per week. The aerobic base builds the engine. Never race your easy runs — save intensity for Thursday fartlek only.

5.

One Rest Day: Saturday is non-negotiable rest. Six training days means sleep 8–9+ hours is the floor, not the goal. Day B (Wednesday) is deliberately low-CNS to act as a semi-recovery day between runs.

6.

Fuel the Machine: Protein 1.8–2.2 g/kg/day across 4+ meals. Don't skip carbs — glycogen depletion worsens the interference effect. Eat at maintenance or a slight surplus. Creatine 5g/day.

7.

Tempo Key: Eccentric – Pause Bottom – Concentric – Pause Top (e.g., 3-1-1-0). X = Explosive.
