WORKOUT

Hybrid Running Athlete

DAY A: Squat + Pull-Up

Focus: Quad + Ham Legs, Vertical Pull · Core + Prehab

7 exercises 20 working sets ~55 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position (hips + thoracic for squats) — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (hip rotation for squat depth) — Ankle Rockers: 2 × 8/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (ankle dorsiflexion for squat depth and calf work) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Bodyweight Squats: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-0-1-0 (quad activation before back squats) + Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 15, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for pull-ups) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Quad-Dominant Compound
Back Squat
4 × 6Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Break at hips and knees simultaneously — sit between your legs, not back onto a chair. Aim for parallel or just below. Drive through the whole foot. Brace hard — big breath, tight core, the whole way down and up. Same weight all 4 sets. The king of leg exercises — builds quads, glutes, and core in one movement. Heavy squats improve running economy by training your legs to produce force efficiently. The 6th rep pushes time under tension into hypertrophy range — important for a hard leg gainer. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s driving up | no pause at top.

02
Unilateral Ham/Glute
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
3 × 8–10/legTempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 90s

Stand on one leg, hold a dumbbell in the opposite hand. Hinge at the hip — push the free leg straight back as a counterbalance while lowering the dumbbell toward the floor. Keep your back flat and hips square. Drive back up by squeezing the glute. Hamstrings are the thinnest link in a 2-day program — deadlifts alone (3 sets on Day B) fall below the minimum growth threshold. SL RDLs add 3 targeted hamstring sets while training the single-leg balance and hip stability that running demands every stride. Start light — balance is the limiting factor at first. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s driving up | no pause at top.

03
Vertical Pull
Weighted Pull-Ups
4 × 6–8Tempo: 2-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Pronated grip (palms away), slightly wider than shoulders. Drive elbows down to your pockets — initiate with lats, not biceps. Full dead hang at the bottom, chin over bar at the top. No swinging or kipping. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10-20s). Builds the lats and upper back that keep you upright during long runs. 2s lowering | 1s pause at dead hang | explosive pull up | 1s hold at top.

04
Prehab (Adductors)
Copenhagen Side Planks
2 × 20–30s/sideTempo: Isometric HoldRest: 60s

Lie on your side, top leg on a bench with inner thigh resting on the edge. Lift your hips and hold — bottom leg hangs or tucks. This loads the hip adductors isometrically in the frontal plane — the plane running hammers hardest. Directly prevents groin strains and knee valgus during running. Scale: bent knee on bench (easier) → straight leg (harder) → bottom leg raised (hardest). Keep hips stacked, don't rotate.

05
Prehab (Abductors / Glute Med)
Side-Lying Hip Abduction
2 × 15–20/sideTempo: 2-1-1-0Rest: 60s

Lie on your side, bottom leg bent for stability, top leg straight. Lift the top leg to ~45° — lead with the heel, not the toe, to target glute medius over TFL. Control the descent. Glute medius weakness is the #1 root cause of running injuries — ITB syndrome, patellofemoral knee pain, knee valgus, and ankle compensation all trace back here. Copenhagen planks hit adductors (inner hip); this hits abductors (outer hip). Together they complete the frontal-plane hip block that running demands. Add an ankle weight when bodyweight feels easy. 2s lifting | 1s hold at top | 1s lowering | no pause at bottom.

06
Prehab (Achilles)
Standing Calf Raises
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Full stretch at the bottom, full squeeze at the top — pause 2s at both ends. Heavy weight, strict form, no bouncing. Standing position loads the gastrocnemius. This is Achilles tendon loading — the #1 injury prevention exercise for runners. Tendinopathy is the most common running injury, and heavy calf raises are the best insurance. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

07
Core (Hip Flexors)
Hanging Leg Raises
2 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60s

Dead hang, posterior pelvic tilt first, then raise legs. Control the descent — no swinging. Straight legs if possible, bent knees to scale down. The hang itself decompresses the spine. Hip flexor strength directly powers your running knee drive. Placed last so core fatigue doesn't compromise bracing on squats and pull-ups. 2s lowering legs | 1s raising legs | 1s hold at top.

DAY B: Deadlift + Dip

Focus: Ham/Glute + Quad Legs, Upper Push · Core + Prehab

7 exercises 23 working sets ~55 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position (hips + thoracic for deadlift) — Cat-Cow: 2 × 8, Tempo: 3-0-3-0 (spinal segmentation for the hinge pattern) — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (hip rotation) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Glute Bridges: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-1-1-2 (glute and hamstring activation before deadlifts) + Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 15, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for dips) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Hip-Dominant Compound
Barbell Deadlift
3 × 5Tempo: 3-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot. Hinge at hips, grip outside knees. Brace hard, push the floor away. Keep the bar in contact with your legs the entire pull. Stand tall at lockout — squeeze glutes, don't hyperextend. Same weight all 3 sets. The deadlift builds the entire posterior chain — hamstrings, glutes, spinal erectors, traps, grip. Strong hamstrings and glutes are your running injury insurance and power your stride. 3 sets keeps the stimulus without the CNS cost that a 4th heavy set adds — critical because Sunday is your long run. 3s lowering | 1s pause at floor | explosive drive | 1s lockout.

02
Unilateral Quad Compound
Bulgarian Split Squats
3 × 8–10/legTempo: 2-1-1-0Rest: 90s

Rear foot elevated on a bench, front foot far enough forward that your knee tracks over toes without passing them. Lower until back knee nearly touches the floor. Drive up through the front heel. Hold dumbbells at your sides. Running is a single-leg activity — squats are bilateral and don't train the single-leg stability, balance, and unilateral strength your stride demands. BSS fills that gap directly while adding quad hypertrophy volume — critical for a hard leg gainer. Placed on Day B so quads get trained 2x/week (Squat Mon + BSS Fri), matching the 2x/week hamstring frequency (SL RDL Mon + Deadlift Fri). 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s driving up | no pause at top.

03
Horizontal Pull
DB Row
4 × 8–10Tempo: 2-1-1-1Rest: 90s

One knee and hand on a bench, other foot on the floor. Pull the dumbbell to your hip — drive the elbow back, don't shrug. Squeeze the shoulder blade at the top, control the descent. Pull-ups hit lats (vertical pull), rows hit rhomboids and mid/lower traps (horizontal pull). Your mid-back is what keeps you upright on long runs — when it fatigues you hunch forward, breathing degrades, everything breaks down. Face pulls are prehab-weight; rows build the real mid-back strength and endurance. 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pulling to hip | 1s squeeze at top.

04
Upper Push
Weighted Dips
4 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Lean forward ~30° to hit chest and front delts, or stay upright for more tricep emphasis. Shoulders down and back, elbows track slightly outward. Lower until upper arm is parallel to floor, drive up. Dips are the upper body squat — they hit chest, triceps, and front delts in one compound movement with no wasted volume. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pressing up | no pause at top.

05
Prehab (Shoulders)
Face Pulls
3 × 12–15Tempo: 2-1-1-2Rest: 60s

Pull towards your eyes. Rotate hands back at the end (double biceps pose). This trains the rear delts and external rotators that counterbalance all the forward-shoulder posture from running and dips. Non-negotiable for shoulder health. 2s letting rope return | 1s pause | 1s pulling to face | 2s squeeze in external rotation.

06
Prehab (Achilles)
Seated Calf Raises
3 × 10–15Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Knee bent at 90° loads the soleus — the deeper, wider calf muscle that sits underneath the gastrocnemius. The soleus is the primary calf muscle used at endurance pace, making this the more running-specific variation. Day A standing hits gastroc, Day B seated hits soleus — full calf coverage across the week. Full stretch at bottom, full squeeze at top. Pause at both ends. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

07
Core (Anti-Rotation)
Pallof Press
2 × 10/sideTempo: 1-2-1-0Rest: 60s

Cable at chest height, stand perpendicular to the machine. Press the handle straight out from your chest and hold — resist the rotation. The further you extend your arms, the harder it gets. Keep hips and shoulders square, brace your whole trunk. This trains the anti-rotation your core needs every running stride — your trunk resists rotation thousands of times per run. Placed last so core fatigue doesn't compromise bracing on deadlifts and dips. 1s pressing out | 2s hold at full extension | 1s returning to chest.

RUN DAYS: Tue · Thu · Sun

Easy Run · Fartlek · Long Run — 80/20 Polarized

3 runs/week 80/20 polarized ~95–115 min/week
WARM-UP (every run): Ankle Circles: 10/side — Single Leg Calf Raises: 10/side (slow & controlled) — Wall Tibialis Raises: 15-25 reps (back against wall, pull toes up, lower slowly — shin splint prevention) — Elephant Walk: 10/side — Leg Swings Front-to-Back: 10/side — Leg Swings Side-to-Side: 10/side
TUE
Recovery Run (Post-Squat)
Easy Run
25–30 minRPE 3 · Zone 2HR: 120–150 bpm

Post-squat day — legs will be sore and that's normal. Walk the first 5-10 minutes if stiff. Genuinely easy: you should hold a full conversation without gasping. If HR drifts above 150, slow down or walk. This is the shortest, easiest run of the week. Walk breaks are always OK. The goal is blood flow and aerobic base, not performance. Easy running post-legs actually speeds recovery by increasing circulation.

THU
Quality Day (Freshest Legs)
Fartlek
30–35 min totalPickups: RPE 6-7Recovery: RPE 3

Freshest running legs of the week — 72 hours post-squat, full rest day yesterday. Structure: 10 min easy warm-up jog → 8 × (45s pickup / 90s easy jog) → 10 min easy cooldown. Pickups are "comfortably faster" — not sprinting, not racing. Go by feel, not pace. If legs feel flat or sore, skip the pickups and make it another easy run — the fartlek is earned, not mandatory. Total hard running is only ~6 minutes out of the whole session.

SUN
Endurance Day (Post-Rest)
Long Run
40–50 minRPE 3-4 · Zone 2Easiest pace of the week

Fresh legs — full rest day Saturday, 48 hours post-deadlift. This is the longest run of the week: time on feet matters, speed doesn't. Run slower than you think you should. Walk breaks are encouraged, not a failure. Build duration gradually — add 5 minutes every 2 weeks. No time pressure, open schedule. Post-run: 5 min walk cooldown, then calf stretch + hip flexor stretch (30s each side).

POST
After Every Run
Cooldown
~5 minWalk + Static Stretches, 30s each

5 min easy walk to bring HR down. Then static stretches: Standing Quad Stretch 30s/side — Standing Calf Stretch (straight leg for gastroc, bent knee for soleus) 30s each/side — Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch 30s/side — Hamstring stretch or standing toe touch 30s. Especially important after Tuesday and Thursday sessions.

B2

B4

Running Rules

1.

Talk Test Is King: If you can't hold a conversation, slow down or walk. HR target 120-150 bpm on easy days. RPE is primary, HR is confirmation.

2.

80/20 Polarized: 2 easy runs (Tue + Sun) + 1 quality session (Thu). Never turn an easy day into a hard day — that's the #1 beginner mistake.

3.

Fartlek Is Earned: Thursday pickups are by feel, not pace. If legs feel flat or sore, skip pickups and run easy. Consistency beats intensity.

4.

Pain Rule: Sharp or localized pain (shin, Achilles, knee) = drop to 2 runs that week. Cut Tuesday, keep Thursday easy + Sunday.

5.

10% Rule: Never increase total weekly running time by more than 10% per week. Tendons and bones adapt slower than lungs.

6.

Form Cues: Land under your hips, not out front. Run quietly — loud footfalls mean overstriding. Relax shoulders, hands loose. Eyes forward 20-30m ahead.

Overtraining Warning Signs

⚠

Physical: Resting HR elevated 7-10+ bpm above baseline. Same pace feels harder than usual (RPE creep). Persistent heavy/sore muscles beyond normal DOMS (>72 hrs). Recurring colds or upper respiratory infections. Unexplained weight loss.

⚠

Mental: Loss of motivation to train. Mood disturbances — irritability, anxiety, feeling flat. Poor sleep despite fatigue. Loss of appetite.

⚠

Action: If 2+ signs persist for more than 1 week, take a full rest week — no lifting, no running. Sleep, eat well, walk if you want. Concurrent training (lifting + running) compounds recovery demands. Catching it early prevents weeks of forced rest later.

Cooldown (Every Lifting Session)

Dead Hangs2–3 × 30–45sRest: 60s

Decompress the spine after every session. Relax everything — shoulders, lats, hips. Grip work is a bonus.

Block 1 — Lifting Protocol (Weeks 1–4)

1.

Weeks 1–3 — Progressive Overload: When you hit the top of the rep range on ALL sets of an exercise, add weight next session (2.5 kg upper, 5 kg lower). If you miss reps, stay at the same weight.

2.

Week 4 — Deload: Same exercises, same weight. Cut sets in half — 4→2, 3→2, 2→1. Keep intensity, slash volume. You will come back stronger. Run deload matches (see Run tab).

3.

Priority This Block: Running is the priority. Lifting supports running performance and prevents injury. If a lift makes you too sore to run the next day, reduce lifting weight by 10% until recovery catches up.

The Hybrid Running Athlete Rules

1.

Run First, Lift to Support: Lifting exists to make you a stronger, more injury-proof runner. Every exercise in this program directly serves your running or prevents a running injury.

2.

6 Compounds + Prehab: Squat, BSS, Deadlift, SL RDL, Pull-Up, Row, Dip. These build total-body strength with minimum fatigue. Everything else is core, prehab, and shoulder health — no fluff.

3.

80/20 Running: 2 easy runs + 1 quality session per week. The aerobic base builds the engine. Never race your easy runs — save intensity for Thursday fartlek only.

4.

Two Rest Days: Wednesday and Saturday are non-negotiable rest. Sleep 8–9+ hours — concurrent training demands more recovery than either alone.

5.

Fuel the Machine: Protein 1.8–2.2 g/kg/day across 4+ meals. Don't skip carbs — glycogen depletion worsens the interference effect. Eat at maintenance or a slight surplus.

6.

Tempo Key: Eccentric – Pause Bottom – Concentric – Pause Top (e.g., 3-1-1-0). X = Explosive.
