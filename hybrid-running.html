WORKOUT

Hybrid Athlete — Built to Lift & Run

DAY A: Quads + Vertical Push/Pull

Focus: Quads, Vertical Push, Vertical Pull · Calves + Side Delts + Rear Delts

7 exercises 25 working sets ~75 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position (hips + thoracic for dips and chin-ups) — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (hip rotation for leg press) — Ankle Rockers: 2 × 8/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (ankle dorsiflexion for squat depth and calf work) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for dips and chin-ups) + Bodyweight Squats: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-0-1-0 (quad activation before leg press) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core
Hanging Leg Raises
2 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60s

Dead hang, posterior pelvic tilt first, then raise legs. Control the descent — no swinging. Straight legs if possible, bent knees to scale down. The hang itself decompresses the spine. Hip flexor strength directly powers your running knee drive. 2s lowering legs | 1s raising legs | 1s hold at top.

02
Quads
Leg Press
3 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Lower the sled until knees hit 90° — full ROM, no half reps. Drive through the whole foot. Don't lock out knees at the top. Same weight across all 3 sets — pick a load where set 3 is a grind. 3s lowering | 1s pause | 1s up | no pause.

03
Quad Isolation
Leg Extensions
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-2Rest: 90s

Adjust pad so it sits on your lower shin, just above the ankle. Extend fully and squeeze the quad hard at the top — hold 2s. Slow negative, don't let the weight drop. Isolates the quads in a higher rep range that compounds don't cover, bringing quad volume above the minimum effective threshold. 2s lowering | 1s extending | 2s squeeze at lockout.

04
Vertical Push
Weighted Dips (Lean Forward)
3 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Lean forward ~30° to shift emphasis to chest and front delts. Shoulders down and back, elbows track slightly outward. Lower until upper arm is parallel to floor, drive up. Dips are the upper body squat — chest, triceps, and front delts in one compound movement. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pressing up | no pause at top.

05
Vertical Pull
Weighted Chin-Ups
4 × 6–8Tempo: 2-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Drive elbows down to your pockets. No swinging or kipping. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10–20s) — isometric peak contraction for lat density. Builds the lats and upper back that keep you upright during long runs. 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | explosive pull up | 1s hold at top.

06
Calves + Side Delts Superset
Standing Calf Raises + Lateral Raises
4 × 8–10 / 3 × 12–15Tempo: 2-2-1-2 / 2-0-1-1Rest: 90s after pair

Calf Raises: Full stretch at the bottom, full squeeze at the top — pause 2s at both ends. Heavy weight, strict form, no bouncing. Standing position loads the gastrocnemius — Achilles tendon loading, the #1 injury prevention exercise for runners. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top. Lateral Raises: Slight forward lean, lead with elbows, raise to just above shoulder height. Side delts build the V-taper and are only hit by direct isolation — no compound covers them. 2s lowering | 1s raising | 1s hold at top.

07
Rear Delts / Posture
Face Pulls
3 × 12–15Tempo: 2-1-1-2Rest: 60s

Pull towards your eyes. Rotate hands back at the end (double biceps pose). Trains rear delts and external rotators that counterbalance forward-shoulder posture from running and pressing. Non-negotiable for shoulder health. 2s letting rope return | 1s pause | 1s pulling to face | 2s squeeze in external rotation.

DAY B: Hams/Glutes + Horizontal Push/Pull

Focus: Hamstrings, Glutes, Horizontal Push, Horizontal Pull · Core + Calves + Side Delts

8 exercises 25 working sets ~75 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 — Cat-Cow: 2 × 8, Tempo: 3-0-3-0 (spinal segmentation for the hinge pattern) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder health for bench and rows) + Glute Bridges: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-1-1-2 (glute and hamstring activation for RDLs and hip thrusts) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core (Anti-Rotation)
Pallof Press
2 × 10/sideTempo: 1-2-1-0Rest: 60s

Cable at chest height, stand perpendicular to the machine. Press the handle straight out from your chest and hold — resist the rotation. The further you extend your arms, the harder it gets. Keep hips and shoulders square, brace your whole trunk. Trains the anti-rotation your core needs every running stride — your trunk resists rotation thousands of times per run. 1s pressing out | 2s hold at full extension | 1s returning to chest.

02
Hip Stability (Frontal Plane)
Copenhagen Side Planks
2 × 20–30s/sideTempo: Isometric HoldRest: 60s

Lie on your side, top leg on a bench with inner thigh resting on the edge. Lift your hips and hold — bottom leg hangs or tucks. Loads the hip adductors isometrically in the frontal plane — directly prevents groin strains and knee valgus during running. Scale: bent knee on bench (easier) → straight leg (harder) → bottom leg raised (hardest). Keep hips stacked, don't rotate.

03
Posterior Chain
Barbell Romanian Deadlift
3 × 5Tempo: 3-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Hinge at hips, push them back. Bar drags along your thighs — keep it in contact the whole way down. Slight knee bend, feel the deep hamstring stretch at the bottom — drive hips forward to lockout. Same weight all 3 sets — heavy but controlled. Strong hamstrings and glutes are your running injury insurance and power your stride. 3s lowering | 1s pause at stretch | explosive drive | 1s lockout.

04
Glutes
Barbell Hip Thrusts
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-0-1-2Rest: 2m

Upper back on bench, feet flat, shin vertical at the top. Drive through heels, squeeze glutes hard at lockout — hold 2s. Don't hyperextend the lower back — think "ribs down, belt buckle to chin." The #1 glute builder and your running injury insurance. Protects knees, powers uphill running. 2s lowering hips | 1s driving up | 2s squeeze at lockout.

05
Hamstring Isolation
Lying Leg Curls
2 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 90s

Lie face down, pad just above the ankles. Curl the weight up by squeezing your hamstrings — don't lift your hips off the pad. Slow negative, squeeze hard at the top. Isolates the hamstrings without glute or lower back involvement — fills the gap that RDLs and hip thrusts don't cover. 2s lowering | 1s curling up | 1s squeeze at top.

06
Horizontal Push
Incline Bench Press
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-1-1-0Rest: 2m

Bar to upper chest (below collarbone). Control the descent strictly. Same weight all 3 sets — grind it out. Pairs with forward-lean dips on Day A for complete chest coverage across the week. 2s lowering bar to chest | 1s pause at chest | 1s pressing up | no pause at lockout.

07
Horizontal Pull
Seated Cable Rows
4 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 2m

Sit tall, chest up, feet braced. Pull handle to lower ribcage, squeeze shoulder blades together. Don't lean back — torso stays upright throughout. Chin-ups hit lats (vertical pull on Day A), rows hit rhomboids and mid/lower traps (horizontal pull). Your mid-back keeps you upright on long runs — when it fatigues you hunch forward and breathing degrades. 2s releasing cable forward | 1s pulling to ribcage | 1s squeeze at top.

08
Calves + Side Delts Superset
Seated Calf Raises + Lateral Raises
3 × 10–15 / 3 × 12–15Tempo: 2-2-1-2 / 2-0-1-1Rest: 90s after pair

Seated Calf Raises: Knee bent at 90° loads the soleus — the deeper, wider calf muscle underneath the gastrocnemius. The soleus is the primary calf muscle used at endurance pace, making this the more running-specific variation. Day A standing hits gastroc, Day B seated hits soleus — full calf coverage. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top. Lateral Raises: Slight forward lean, lead with elbows, raise to just above shoulder height. Second weekly dose for side delts — volume matched with Day A for 6 total weekly sets. 2s lowering | 1s raising | 1s hold at top.

RUN DAYS: Tue · Thu · Sun

Easy Run · Fartlek · Long Run — 80/20 Polarized

3 runs/week 80/20 polarized ~95–115 min/week
WARM-UP (every run): Ankle Circles: 10/side — Single Leg Calf Raises: 10/side (slow & controlled) — Wall Tibialis Raises: 15-25 reps (back against wall, pull toes up, lower slowly — shin splint prevention) — Elephant Walk: 10/side — Leg Swings Front-to-Back: 10/side — Leg Swings Side-to-Side: 10/side
TUE
Recovery Run (Post-Leg Press)
Easy Run
25–30 minRPE 3 · Zone 2HR: 120–150 bpm

Post-quad day — legs will be sore and that's normal. Walk the first 5-10 minutes if stiff. Genuinely easy: you should hold a full conversation without gasping. If HR drifts above 150, slow down or walk. This is the shortest, easiest run of the week. Walk breaks are always OK. The goal is blood flow and aerobic base, not performance. Easy running post-legs actually speeds recovery by increasing circulation.

THU
Quality Day (Freshest Legs)
Fartlek
30–35 min totalPickups: RPE 6-7Recovery: RPE 3

Freshest running legs of the week — 72 hours post-Day A, full rest day yesterday. Structure: 10 min easy warm-up jog → 8 × (45s pickup / 90s easy jog) → 10 min easy cooldown. Pickups are "comfortably faster" — not sprinting, not racing. Go by feel, not pace. If legs feel flat or sore, skip the pickups and make it another easy run — the fartlek is earned, not mandatory. Total hard running is only ~6 minutes out of the whole session.

SUN
Endurance Day (Post-Rest)
Long Run
40–50 minRPE 3-4 · Zone 2Easiest pace of the week

Fresh legs — full rest day Saturday, 48 hours post-Day B. This is the longest run of the week: time on feet matters, speed doesn't. Run slower than you think you should. Walk breaks are encouraged, not a failure. Build duration gradually — add 5 minutes every 2 weeks. No time pressure, open schedule. Post-run: 5 min walk cooldown, then calf stretch + hip flexor stretch (30s each side).

POST

Then static stretches: Standing Quad Stretch 30s/side — Standing Calf Stretch (straight leg for gastroc, bent knee for soleus) 30s each/side — Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch 30s/side — Hamstring stretch or standing toe touch 30s. Running Rules

1.

Talk Test Is King: If you can't hold a conversation, slow down or walk. HR target 120-150 bpm on easy days. RPE is primary, HR is confirmation.

2.

80/20 Polarized: 2 easy runs (Tue + Sun) + 1 quality session (Thu). Never turn an easy day into a hard day — that's the #1 beginner mistake.

3.

Fartlek Is Earned: Thursday pickups are by feel, not pace. If legs feel flat or sore, skip pickups and run easy. Consistency beats intensity.

4.

Pain Rule: Sharp or localized pain (shin, Achilles, knee) = drop to 2 runs that week. Cut Tuesday, keep Thursday easy + Sunday.

5.

10% Rule: Never increase total weekly running time by more than 10% per week. Tendons and bones adapt slower than lungs.

6.

Form Cues: Land under your hips, not out front. Run quietly — loud footfalls mean overstriding. Relax shoulders, hands loose. Eyes forward 20-30m ahead.

Overtraining Warning Signs

⚠

Physical: Resting HR elevated 7-10+ bpm above baseline. Same pace feels harder than usual (RPE creep). Persistent heavy/sore muscles beyond normal DOMS (>72 hrs). Recurring colds or upper respiratory infections. Unexplained weight loss.

⚠

Mental: Loss of motivation to train. Mood disturbances — irritability, anxiety, feeling flat. Poor sleep despite fatigue. Loss of appetite.

⚠

Action: If 2+ signs persist for more than 1 week, take a full rest week — no lifting, no running. Sleep, eat well, walk if you want. Concurrent training (lifting + running) compounds recovery demands. Catching it early prevents weeks of forced rest later.

Cooldown (Every Lifting Session)

Dead Hangs2–3 × 30–45sRest: 60s

Decompress the spine after every session. Relax everything — shoulders, lats, hips. Grip work is a bonus.

Block 1 — Lifting Protocol (Weeks 1–4)

1.

Weeks 1–3 — Progressive Overload: When you hit the top of the rep range on ALL sets of an exercise, add weight next session (2.5 kg upper, 5 kg lower). If you miss reps, stay at the same weight.

2.

Week 4 — Deload: Same exercises, same weight. Cut sets in half — 4→2, 3→2, 2→1. Keep intensity, slash volume. You will come back stronger. Run deload matches (see Run tab).

3.

Neither Owns the Other: Lifting and running are equal priorities. If a lift makes you too sore to run the next day, reduce lifting weight by 10%. If a run leaves you too fatigued to lift, shorten the run. Both must coexist — adjust whichever is out of balance.

The Hybrid Athlete Rules

1.

Not a Bodybuilder Who Runs. Not a Runner Who Lifts: You are a hybrid athlete — the best of both worlds working in sync. Lifting builds the dense, balanced, athletic physique. Running builds the cardiovascular engine, VO2Max, and keeps you lean. Neither is subordinate. Both serve the whole.

2.

The Goal: A good-looking, balanced, healthy, functional, flexible, toned, dense and well-built body that can comfortably run 10-15K with strong lungs and a powerful cardiovascular system. Not a marathon. Not a bodybuilding stage. The athletic hybrid.

3.

Compounds + Targeted Isolation: Leg Press, Dips, Chin-Ups, RDL, Hip Thrusts, Incline Bench, Cable Rows. Vertical + horizontal push and pull covered. Leg Extensions + Leg Curls for isolation volume. Face Pulls, Lateral Raises, Copenhagens, and Calves for prehab and shoulder health — no fluff.

4.

80/20 Running: 2 easy runs + 1 quality session per week. The aerobic base builds the engine. Never race your easy runs — save intensity for Thursday fartlek only.

5.

Two Rest Days: Wednesday and Saturday are non-negotiable rest. Sleep 8–9+ hours — concurrent training demands more recovery than either alone.

6.

Fuel the Machine: Protein 1.8–2.2 g/kg/day across 4+ meals. Don't skip carbs — glycogen depletion worsens the interference effect. Eat at maintenance or a slight surplus. Creatine 5g/day.

7.

Tempo Key: Eccentric – Pause Bottom – Concentric – Pause Top (e.g., 3-1-1-0). X = Explosive.
