WORKOUT

Hybrid Running Athlete

DAY A: Squat + Pull-Up

Focus: Quad-Dominant Legs, Vertical Pull · Core + Prehab

5 exercises 15 working sets ~40 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position (hips + thoracic for squats) — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (hip rotation for squat depth) — Ankle Rockers: 2 × 8/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (ankle dorsiflexion for squat depth and calf work) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Bodyweight Squats: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-0-1-0 (quad activation before back squats) + Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 15, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for pull-ups) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core (Hip Flexors)
Hanging Leg Raises
2 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60s

Dead hang, posterior pelvic tilt first, then raise legs. Control the descent — no swinging. Straight legs if possible, bent knees to scale down. The hang itself decompresses the spine. Hip flexor strength directly powers your running knee drive. 2s lowering legs | 1s raising legs | 1s hold at top.

02
Quad-Dominant Compound
Back Squat
4 × 6Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Break at hips and knees simultaneously — sit between your legs, not back onto a chair. Aim for parallel or just below. Drive through the whole foot. Brace hard — big breath, tight core, the whole way down and up. Same weight all 4 sets. The king of leg exercises — builds quads, glutes, and core in one movement. Heavy squats improve running economy by training your legs to produce force efficiently. The 6th rep pushes time under tension into hypertrophy range — important for a hard leg gainer. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s driving up | no pause at top.

03
Vertical Pull
Weighted Pull-Ups
4 × 6–8Tempo: 2-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Pronated grip (palms away), slightly wider than shoulders. Drive elbows down to your pockets — initiate with lats, not biceps. Full dead hang at the bottom, chin over bar at the top. No swinging or kipping. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10-20s). Builds the lats and upper back that keep you upright during long runs. 2s lowering | 1s pause at dead hang | explosive pull up | 1s hold at top.

04
Prehab (Adductors)
Copenhagen Side Planks
2 × 20–30s/sideTempo: Isometric HoldRest: 60s

Lie on your side, top leg on a bench with inner thigh resting on the edge. Lift your hips and hold — bottom leg hangs or tucks. This loads the hip adductors isometrically in the frontal plane — the plane running hammers hardest. Directly prevents groin strains and knee valgus during running. Scale: bent knee on bench (easier) → straight leg (harder) → bottom leg raised (hardest). Keep hips stacked, don't rotate.

05
Prehab (Achilles)
Standing Calf Raises
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Full stretch at the bottom, full squeeze at the top — pause 2s at both ends. Heavy weight, strict form, no bouncing. Standing position loads the gastrocnemius. This is Achilles tendon loading — the #1 injury prevention exercise for runners. Tendinopathy is the most common running injury, and heavy calf raises are the best insurance. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

DAY B: Deadlift + Dip

Focus: Hip-Dominant Legs, Upper Push · Core + Prehab

5 exercises 15 working sets ~40 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position (hips + thoracic for deadlift) — Cat-Cow: 2 × 8, Tempo: 3-0-3-0 (spinal segmentation for the hinge pattern) — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (hip rotation) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Glute Bridges: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-1-1-2 (glute and hamstring activation before deadlifts) + Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 15, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for dips) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core (Anti-Rotation)
Pallof Press
2 × 10/sideTempo: 1-2-1-0Rest: 60s

Cable at chest height, stand perpendicular to the machine. Press the handle straight out from your chest and hold — resist the rotation. The further you extend your arms, the harder it gets. Keep hips and shoulders square, brace your whole trunk. This trains the anti-rotation your core needs every running stride — your trunk resists rotation thousands of times per run. 1s pressing out | 2s hold at full extension | 1s returning to chest.

02
Hip-Dominant Compound
Barbell Deadlift
3 × 5Tempo: 3-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot. Hinge at hips, grip outside knees. Brace hard, push the floor away. Keep the bar in contact with your legs the entire pull. Stand tall at lockout — squeeze glutes, don't hyperextend. Same weight all 3 sets. The deadlift builds the entire posterior chain — hamstrings, glutes, spinal erectors, traps, grip. Strong hamstrings and glutes are your running injury insurance and power your stride. 3 sets keeps the stimulus without the CNS cost that a 4th heavy set adds — critical because Friday is your long run. 3s lowering | 1s pause at floor | explosive drive | 1s lockout.

03
Upper Push
Weighted Dips
4 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Lean forward ~30° to hit chest and front delts, or stay upright for more tricep emphasis. Shoulders down and back, elbows track slightly outward. Lower until upper arm is parallel to floor, drive up. Dips are the upper body squat — they hit chest, triceps, and front delts in one compound movement with no wasted volume. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pressing up | no pause at top.

04
Prehab (Shoulders)
Face Pulls
3 × 12–15Tempo: 2-1-1-2Rest: 60s

Pull towards your eyes. Rotate hands back at the end (double biceps pose). This trains the rear delts and external rotators that counterbalance all the forward-shoulder posture from running and dips. Non-negotiable for shoulder health. 2s letting rope return | 1s pause | 1s pulling to face | 2s squeeze in external rotation.

05
Prehab (Achilles)
Standing Calf Raises
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Same protocol as Day A. Full stretch, full squeeze, pause at both ends. Heavy and strict. Runners need calf raises on both lifting days — the Achilles tendon absorbs 6-8x bodyweight per stride. Loading it twice a week builds the resilience to handle 180 minutes of running. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

01
Pre-Run
Dynamic Warm-Up
~3 minTempo: Controlled

Leg Swings: 10/side (front-to-back + side-to-side). Walking Lunges: 10 steps. High Knees: 20 steps. Ankle circles: 10/side. Don't static stretch cold muscles — save that for after.

02
The Session
Walk/Run Intervals
See weekly progression aboveFriday = "long" session (slightly longer)

Walk briskly during walk phases — don't stroll. Run at a conversational pace during run phases — you should be able to speak in full sentences. If you can't talk, you're going too fast. Stay tall — don't hunch forward. Land midfoot, not on your heels. The walk/run ratio progresses each week — same easy effort, just more time running. Friday is always the longest session to build endurance gradually. If a week feels hard, repeat it instead of progressing — consistency beats speed.

03
Post-Run
Cooldown Stretches
~5 minTempo: Static Holds, 30s each

Standing Quad Stretch: 30s/side. Standing Calf Stretch (straight leg for gastroc, bent knee for soleus): 30s each/side. Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch: 30s/side — same as your APT correction stretch, doubles as recovery. Hamstring doorway stretch or standing toe touch: 30s.

Phase 1 — Build the Base (You Are Here · Block 1 of ~3)

1.

Block 1 (now): Walk 1:30 / Run 1:00 → progressing to Walk 1:00 / Run 1:30 by Week 3. Deload Week 4.

2.

Block 2 (next): Walk 0:45 / Run 2:00 → Walk 0:30 / Run 2:30. Introduce Friday as a dedicated longer session.

3.

Block 3: Walk 0:30 / Run 3:00 → continuous running. Friday long run reaches 30+ min nonstop.

4.

Phase 1 complete when: You can run 30 min continuously at a conversational pace with no walk breaks.

Phase 2 — Build Mileage (3 Runs/Week)

5.

Structure: Tue = easy run (30 min) — Wed = easy run (30 min) — Fri = long run (start at 40 min, add 5 min every 2 weeks)

6.

All runs are easy: Zone 2, conversational pace. The long run is longer, not faster. Speed comes later — aerobic base comes first.

7.

10% rule: Never increase total weekly running time by more than 10% per week. Tendons and bones adapt slower than your lungs.

8.

Phase 2 complete when: Your long run reaches 75–80 min continuously (~8–9 km at easy pace)

Phase 3 — Half Marathon Ready

9.

Structure: Tue = easy run (30–40 min) — Wed = easy run (30–40 min) — Fri = long run (build to 100–110 min)

10.

Peak long run: Once you can run 100–110 min continuously (roughly 16–18 km at easy pace), you are half marathon ready. You don't need to run 21 km in training.

11.

Race week: Cut all running volume in half the week before race day. Lift normally. Rest Friday and Saturday. Race Sunday.

Cooldown (Every Lifting Session)

Dead Hangs2–3 × 30–45sRest: 60s

Decompress the spine after every session. Relax everything — shoulders, lats, hips. Grip work is a bonus.

Block 1 — Lifting Protocol (Weeks 1–4)

1.

Weeks 1–3 — Progressive Overload: When you hit the top of the rep range on ALL sets of an exercise, add weight next session (2.5 kg upper, 5 kg lower). If you miss reps, stay at the same weight.

2.

Week 4 — Deload: Same exercises, same weight. Cut sets in half — 4→2, 3→2, 2→1. Keep intensity, slash volume. You will come back stronger. Run deload matches (see Run tab).

3.

Priority This Block: Running is the priority. Lifting supports running performance and prevents injury. If a lift makes you too sore to run the next day, reduce lifting weight by 10% until recovery catches up.

The Hybrid Running Athlete Rules

1.

Run First, Lift to Support: Lifting exists to make you a stronger, more injury-proof runner. Every exercise in this program directly serves your running or prevents a running injury.

2.

4 Compounds, Nothing Else: Squat, Deadlift, Pull-Up, Dip. These build total-body strength with minimum fatigue. Everything else is core and prehab — no isolation, no fluff.

3.

Run Easy: All running is Zone 2 — conversational pace. The aerobic base builds the engine. Never race your easy runs.

4.

Two Rest Days: Saturday and Sunday are non-negotiable rest. Sleep 8–9+ hours — concurrent training demands more recovery than either alone.

5.

Fuel the Machine: Protein 1.8–2.2 g/kg/day across 4+ meals. Don't skip carbs — glycogen depletion worsens the interference effect. Eat at maintenance or a slight surplus.

6.

Tempo Key: Eccentric – Pause Bottom – Concentric – Pause Top (e.g., 3-1-1-0). X = Explosive.
