WORKOUT

The Final Cut — Refined

Tall. Toned. Athletic. Built for the lean V-taper — big arms, slim waist, chiseled abs, great yoke.

DAY A: Vertical Power & Drive

Focus: Legs, Upper Chest, Athletic Arms

MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 5/side — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side (hip internal/external rotation for leg press depth and hip thrusts) — Wall Slides: 2 × 8 (scapular upward rotation + shoulder flexion for pressing and chin-ups)
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20 + Light Lateral Raises: 2 × 15–20 (activates rear delts & lateral delts — primes shoulders for pressing and builds delt cap volume across the week)
01
Primer
Explosive Goblet Squats
3 × 5Tempo: Explosive ResetRest: 60–90s

Light DB or KB held at chest. Squat to full depth, then drive up as fast and hard as possible. Feet stay on the floor. Reset fully between reps — max intent every rep.

02
Main Strength
Leg Press
1 Top Set (6–8) + 2 Back-Off (10–12)Tempo: 3-1-1-0 / Back-Off: 5-1-1-0Rest: 3–5m / 3m

Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Lower the sled until knees hit 90° — full ROM, no half reps. Drive through the whole foot. Go brutally heavy on the top set. Don't lock out knees at the top. Top Set: 3s lowering | 1s pause | 1s up | no pause. Back-Off: 5s slow eccentric | 1s pause | 1s up | no pause — slower eccentric on lighter sets builds longer, denser muscle fibers.

03
Upper Push
Incline Bench Press
3 × 8–10Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Bar to upper chest (below collarbone). Control the descent strictly. 3s lowering bar to chest | 1s pause at chest | 1s pressing up | no pause at lockout.

04
Upper Pull
Weighted Chin-Ups
3 × 6–8Tempo: 2-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Drive elbows down to your pockets. No swinging or kipping. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10–20s) — isometric peak contraction for lat density (GST principle). 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | explosive pull up | 1s hold at top.

05
Glutes (Isolation)
Barbell Hip Thrusts
2 × 10–12Tempo: 2-2-1-0Rest: 90s

Drive through heels, squeeze glutes hard at lockout for 2s. Don't hyperextend the lower back — stop when hips are level. Chin tucked. 2s lowering hips | 2s pause at bottom | 1s driving up to lockout | no pause at top.

06
Single-Leg Strength
DB Step-Ups
2 × 8–10/legTempo: 2-1-1-1Rest: 90s

Use a 12–16 inch box (knee height). DBs hang at sides. Drive through the heel of the top foot — the back foot is a ghost (light touch-and-go, no push-off). Stand tall at top: full hip extension, 1s glute squeeze. Control the descent. All reps on one leg, then switch. 2s stepping up | 1s standing tall at top | 1s stepping down | 1s pause at bottom.

07
Arms Superset
Overhead Triceps Extensions + Incline DB Curls
3 × 12–15 / 3 × 10–12Tempo: 3-1-1-0 / 3-0-1-1Rest: 60–90s after pair

Triceps: Elbows tucked, deep stretch at bottom. Curls: Let the incline stretch the long head fully — this builds the bicep peak. No swinging. Extensions: 3s lowering behind head | 1s pause at stretch | 1s extending up. Curls: 3s lowering to stretch | 1s curling up | 1s squeeze at top.

08
Calves (Strength)
Standing Calf Raises (Heavy)
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Full 2s pause at top AND bottom. Heavy weight, strict form, no bouncing. 2s lowering heels below platform | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

09
Finisher
Heavy Farmer's Carries
3 × 40 yardsTempo: Steady/Fast WalkRest: 2m

Walk tall, chest up. Don't let weights bang legs. Breathe behind the brace.

10
Neck (Flexion)
Plate Neck Curls
3 × 15–20Tempo: 2-1-2-0Rest: 45–60s

Lie face-up on bench, head off the edge. Plate on forehead (use towel). Curl chin to chest slowly. Light weight — 10–25 lbs is plenty. Control every rep. 2s lowering head back | 1s pause at stretch | 2s curling chin to chest.

DAY B: Overhead Power & Speed

Focus: Shoulders, Width, Arms, Athletic Core

MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 5/side — Shoulder CARs: 3/direction/arm (full shoulder ROM before overhead pressing) — Deep Squat Hold + Thoracic Rotations: 30s hold + 5 rotations/side (thoracic mobility for overhead work, hip opening for pull-throughs and snatches)
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20 + Light Lateral Raises: 2 × 15–20 (same as Day A — free lateral delt volume every session builds the wide-shoulder illusion over time)
01
Primer
Cable Pull-Throughs
3 × 12Tempo: Explosive + Eccentric ControlRest: 60–90s

Face away from cable, rope attachment between legs. Hip hinge back to load hamstrings, then snap hips forward explosively. Resist the pull on the way back — fight the cable with your hamstrings on every eccentric. Same hip hinge pattern and hamstring loading as KB swings.

02
Explosive Power
Dumbbell Power Snatch
3 × 5/armTempo: ExplosiveRest: 90s

Single DB on the floor. Explosive hip drive launches the weight overhead in one motion. Full hip extension at the top. Catch with a locked arm. Reset fully between reps. Athletic, full-body CNS primer before overhead pressing.

03
The Press
Standing Overhead Barbell Press
1 Top Set (3–5) + 2 Back-Off (8–10)Tempo: 2-0-X-0 / Back-Off: 4-0-X-0Rest: 3–5m / 3m

Squeeze glutes to protect lower back. Push head through the window at top. Top Set: 2s lowering | explosive press. Back-Off: 4s slow eccentric | explosive press — controlled eccentric on lighter sets maximizes muscle density.

04
Assistance Pull
Chest-Supported DB Rows
4 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-0Rest: 3m

Set incline bench to 30–45°, lie chest-down. Let DBs hang at full arm extension. Pull elbows back toward hips, squeeze shoulder blades. The chest support eliminates all spinal loading — pure lat and rhomboid work. 2s lowering DBs to full hang | 1s pulling to ribcage.

05
Triceps (Mass)
Weighted Dips (Upright)
3 × 10–15Tempo: 3-0-1-0Rest: 3m

Stay upright — torso vertical, elbows tight to body. This locks it in as a tricep builder. Shoulders down and back. 3s lowering | 1s pressing up.

06
Arms Superset
Strict Barbell Curls + Rope Pushdowns
3 × 10–12 / 3 × 12–15Tempo: 3-0-1-0 / 2-0-1-1Rest: 90s after pair

Curls: Elbows pinned, no swinging. Pushdowns: Split the rope at the bottom, squeeze triceps hard. Lateral head focus = horseshoe shape. Curls: 3s lowering | 1s curling up. Pushdowns: 2s letting rope return | 1s pushing down | 1s squeeze.

07
Width Builder
Lateral Raises (Heavy Working Sets)
3 × 12–15Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60–90s

Slight forward lean, lead with elbows, raise to just above shoulder height. Controlled negative — don't just drop the weight. Go heavier than warm-up laterals, this is for growth. 2s lowering | 1s raising | 1s hold at top.

08
The Shoulders
Lu Raises (Heavy)
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60–90s

Full ROM overhead. Heavier weight, strict control — no drop sets. 2s lowering from overhead | 1s raising overhead | 1s hold at top.

09
Core (GST Hold)
L-Sit Progression Holds
2–3 × Max HoldTempo: IsometricRest: 60–90s

On parallettes, dip bars, or floor: lock arms straight, depress shoulders (push down hard), lift legs to horizontal. Hold with legs straight and together. Progression: feet on floor (support) → tuck L-sit (knees bent) → full L-sit (legs straight). Keep lower back neutral — don't round. Builds extremely dense, hard abs + hip flexors through isometric tension — zero waist thickening, zero spinal flexion.

DAY C: Ground Force & The Yoke

Focus: Total Body, V-Taper, Arms & Yoke

MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 5/side — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side (hip rotation for RDL depth and position) — Cat-Cow: 2 × 8 (spinal segmentation and awareness for the hinge pattern)
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20 + Light Lateral Raises: 2 × 15–20 (every session = ~6 extra lateral delt sets/week. The muscle most responsible for looking wide at the shoulders and narrow at the waist)
01
Primer
Speed Trap Bar Deadlifts
3 × 3Tempo: Explosive ResetRest: 90s

~50–60% 1RM. Pull as fast as possible — max acceleration off the floor. Control the descent (don't drop it). Reset fully between reps. CNS primer for the heavy RDLs that follow.

02
The Monster
Trap Bar Romanian Deadlift
1 Top Set (5) + 2 Back-Off (5, explosive)Tempo: X-1-X-1 / Back-Off: 4-1-X-1Rest: 3–5m

Hinge at hips, push them back. Keep the bar close, slight knee bend. Deep hamstring stretch at the bottom — drive hips forward to lockout. Chest tall throughout. Top Set: explosive hinge down | 1s pause | explosive drive | 1s lockout. Back-Off: 4s controlled eccentric | 1s pause at stretch | explosive drive | 1s lockout — slow eccentric stretches hamstrings under load for longer, denser fibers.

03
Accessory Push
Flat Dumbbell Bench Press
3 × 8–12Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Deep stretch at the bottom by bringing DBs slightly wider than normal. 3s lowering DBs to chest | 1s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up.

04
Accessory Pull
Pull-Ups / Lat Pulldowns
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-1-1-1Rest: 3m

Full dead-hang stretch at bottom. Squeeze lats hard at the top. Build the V-taper. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10–20s) — isometric peak contraction for lat density (GST principle). 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pulling up | 1s hold at top.

05
The Yoke
Hang High Pulls
3 × 6Tempo: ExplosiveRest: 90s

Violent hip extension drives the bar up. Elbows high. Athletic trap builder, not a bodybuilding shrug.

06
Posterior Chain (Knee Flexion)
Lying Leg Curls
2 × 10–12Tempo: 3-1-1-2Rest: 90s

Slow eccentric (3s), pause at bottom stretch (1s), curl up, hard 2s squeeze at top. Don't let hips rise off the pad — weight is too heavy if they do. Trains hamstrings through knee flexion, which the trap bar deadlift doesn't cover. 3s extending legs out | 1s pause at stretch | 1s curling up | 2s squeeze at top.

07
Rear Delts / Posture
Face Pulls
2 × 20Tempo: 2-1-1-2Rest: 60s

Pull towards your eyes. Rotate hands back at the end (double biceps pose). 2s letting rope return | 1s pause | 1s pulling to face | 2s squeeze in external rotation.

08
Arms + Calves Tri-Set
Hammer Curls → Reverse Curls → Seated Calf Raises
3 × 10–12 / 2 × 15 / 3 × 15–20Tempo: 3-0-1-0 / 2-0-1-0 / 2-1-1-2Rest: 60–90s after tri-set

Hammers: Neutral grip, no swinging — builds brachialis (arm width). Reverse Curls: Overhand grip — targets brachioradialis and forearm extensors. Seated Calves: Deep stretch, full squeeze for soleus thickness. Hammers: 3s lowering | 1s curling. Rev. Curls: 2s lowering | 1s curling. Calves: 2s lowering heels | 1s stretch | 1s up | 2s squeeze.

09
Core Shield
Ab Wheel Rollouts
3 × 10–15Tempo: 3-0-1-0Rest: 90s

Round your back slightly (cat pose). Don't let lower back sag or arch. 3s rolling out | 1s pulling back.

10
The Yoke (Neck)
Neck Extensions + Lateral Flexion
Ext: 2 × 15–20 / Lateral: 2 × 12–15/sideTempo: 2-1-2-0Rest: 45–60s

Extensions: Lie face-down on bench, plate on back of head, extend neck up. Lateral: Side-lying or standing with band, tilt ear to shoulder. Keep it light — neck responds to volume, not load. 2s lowering | 1s pause at stretch | 2s lifting.

CONDITIONING: Sprint Session (1x per week, off-day)

Protocol

6–8 rounds: 10s ALL-OUT sprint → 50s easy recovery

Equipment

Assault Bike, Rower, or Hill Sprints

Total Time

Under 10 minutes

Alactic capacity work — same energy system as your explosive primers. Drives leanness, improves recovery between sets on lifting days, and builds the hard athletic look at rest.

The Athletic Rules

1.

Toned at Rest: Heavy loads + explosive lifting = high myogenic tone. Muscles stay hard even when relaxed.

2.

The V-Taper: Built by lats & delts, revealed by leanness. Prioritize pulls equal to pushes.

3.

Density over Pump: Straight heavy sets, no drop sets. Chase neural adaptations, not the pump.

4.

Conditioning = The Look: One sprint session per week accelerates leanness more than any extra gym set.

5.

Tempo Key: Eccentric – Pause Bottom – Concentric – Pause Top (e.g., 3-1-1-0). X = Explosive.

DAILY COOLDOWN (Every Session)

Dead Hangs2–3 × 30–45sRest: 60s

Decompress the spine after every session. Relax everything — shoulders, lats, hips. Grip work is a bonus.

Stomach Vacuums3 × 20–30s holdsRest: 30s

Exhale all air, pull belly button to spine, hold. Trains the transverse abdominis — the internal corset that pulls your waist tight at rest. Do standing or on all fours.

APT CORRECTION (Every Session)

Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch60s/side

Half-kneeling, back knee on pad. Squeeze the glute of the back leg and push hips forward. Lengthens the psoas and rectus femoris — the two muscles that pull the pelvis into anterior tilt.

Dead Bugs2 × 10/sideTempo: SlowRest: 60s

Lie face-up, arms straight to ceiling, knees at 90°. Press lower back flat into the floor (posterior pelvic tilt). Extend opposite arm and leg while keeping the lower back glued to the ground. If your back arches off the floor, you've gone too far.
