DAY A: Quads & Vertical

Focus: Quads, Vertical Push, Vertical Pull · Core + Calves

6 exercises 15 working sets ~65 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position (hips + thoracic for squats and dips) — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (hip rotation for leg press and Bulgarians) — Ankle Rockers: 2 × 8/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (ankle dorsiflexion for squat depth and calf work) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder prep for dips and chin-ups) + Bodyweight Squats: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-0-1-0 (quad activation before leg press) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core
Hanging Leg Raises
2 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60s

Dead hang, posterior pelvic tilt first, then raise legs. Control the descent — no swinging. Straight legs if possible, bent knees to scale down. The hang itself decompresses the spine. 2s lowering legs | 1s raising legs | 1s hold at top.

02
Quads
Leg Press
3 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Lower the sled until knees hit 90° — full ROM, no half reps. Drive through the whole foot. Don't lock out knees at the top. Same weight across all 3 sets — pick a load where set 3 is a grind. Straight sets, no top/back-off complexity. 3s lowering | 1s pause | 1s up | no pause.

03
Single-Leg Strength
Bulgarian Split Squats
2 × 8/legTempo: 2-0-1-0Rest: 60s (alternate legs)

Rear foot elevated on bench, DBs hang at sides. Lower until front thigh is parallel, drive through front heel. This is your running injury insurance — trains the glute medius, fixes bilateral imbalances, and builds the single-leg stability that every running stride demands. No pause at the bottom — controlled down, drive up, keep moving. 2s lowering | 1s driving up.

04
Vertical Push
Weighted Dips (Lean Forward)
3 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Lean forward ~30° to shift emphasis to chest and front delts — different from Day B's upright tricep-focused dips. Shoulders down and back, elbows track slightly outward. Lower until upper arm is parallel to floor, drive up. 3s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pressing up | no pause at top.

05
Vertical Pull
Weighted Chin-Ups
3 × 6–8Tempo: 2-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Drive elbows down to your pockets. No swinging or kipping. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10–20s) — isometric peak contraction for lat density. 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | explosive pull up | 1s hold at top.

06
Calves
Standing Calf Raises
2 × 8–10Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Full stretch at the bottom, full squeeze at the top — pause 2s at both ends. Heavy weight, strict form, no bouncing. Standing position loads the gastrocnemius — the outer calf muscle that makes calves look bigger. 2s lowering heels below platform | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

DAY B: Arms & Shoulders

Focus: Shoulders, Arms, Supplemental Pull · Skippable if recovery demands it

6 exercises 14 working sets ~55 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): Shoulder CARs: 3 rounds per arm (1 forward circle + 1 backward circle = 1 round), Tempo: 5s per circle (full shoulder ROM before dips, pull-ups, and lateral raises) — Quadruped Thoracic Rotations: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2s rotate + 2s hold (on all fours, hand behind head, rotate elbow to ceiling — keep hips square, only the upper back moves. Opens thoracic spine for pull-ups and dips) — Wall Slides: 2 × 8, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 (scapular upward rotation for overhead reach and pressing) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 + Light Lateral Raises: 2 × 15–20, Tempo: 1-0-1-0 (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core
L-Sit Progression Holds
3 × 15–20sTempo: Isometric HoldRest: 60s

Parallel bars or dip handles. Lock arms straight, lift legs parallel to floor and hold. Scale: tuck L-sit (knees bent) → one leg extended → full L-sit. GST isometric — builds dense, flat abs without bulk. Trains rectus abdominis and TVA simultaneously.

02
V-Taper Pull
Pull-Ups / Lat Pulldowns
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-1-1-1Rest: 2m

Full dead-hang stretch at bottom. Squeeze lats hard at the top. Last rep of last set: hold chin over bar for max time (aim 10–20s) — isometric peak contraction for lat density. 2s lowering | 1s pause at bottom | 1s pulling up | 1s hold at top.

03
Vertical Push
Standing Overhead Barbell Press
3 × 6–8Tempo: 2-0-X-0Rest: 3m

Squeeze glutes to protect lower back. Push head through the window at top. Controlled 2s lowering, then explosive press. Same weight all 3 sets. 2s lowering | explosive press up | no pause.

04
Width Builder
Lateral Raises
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 60s

Slight forward lean, lead with elbows, raise to just above shoulder height. Controlled negative — don't just drop the weight. Go heavier than warm-up laterals, this is for growth. 2s lowering | 1s raising | 1s hold at top.

05
Rear Delts / Posture
Face Pulls
2 × 12–15Tempo: 2-1-1-2Rest: 60s

Pull towards your eyes. Rotate hands back at the end (double biceps pose). 2s letting rope return | 1s pause | 1s pulling to face | 2s squeeze in external rotation.

06
Arms Superset
Strict Barbell Curls + Rope Pushdowns
3 × 10–12 / 3 × 10–12Tempo: 3-0-1-0 / 2-0-1-1Rest: 90s after pair

Curls: Elbows pinned, no swinging. Pushdowns: Split the rope at the bottom, squeeze triceps hard. Lateral head focus = horseshoe shape. Curls: 3s lowering | 1s curling up. Pushdowns: 2s letting rope return | 1s pushing down | 1s squeeze.

DAY C: Glutes, Hams & Horizontal

Focus: Glutes, Hamstrings, Horizontal Push, Horizontal Pull · Core + Calves

6 exercises 16 working sets ~70 min total
MOBILITY (~5 min): World's Greatest Stretch: 1 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-3s each position — 90/90 Hip Switches: 2 × 5/side, Tempo: 2-1-2-1 — Cat-Cow: 2 × 8, Tempo: 3-0-3-0 (spinal segmentation for the hinge pattern) — Rest: no rest, flow between drills
WARM-UP: Band Pull-Aparts: 2 × 20, Tempo: 1-0-1-1 (shoulder health for bench and rows) + Glute Bridges: 2 × 10, Tempo: 2-1-1-2 (glute and hamstring activation for RDLs and hip thrusts) (Rest: 30s between sets)
01
Core
Ab Wheel Rollouts
2 × 8–10Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 60s

Kneel on a pad, brace hard, roll out as far as you can control — no lower back sag. Squeeze abs to pull back. Scale: half range → full range → standing rollouts. The king of anti-extension. 3s rolling out | 1s pause at full extension | 1s pulling back | no pause.

02
Posterior Chain
Trap Bar Romanian Deadlift
3 × 5Tempo: 3-1-X-1Rest: 3m

Hinge at hips, push them back. Keep the bar close, slight knee bend. Deep hamstring stretch at the bottom — drive hips forward to lockout. Chest tall throughout. Same weight all 3 sets — heavy but controlled. 3s lowering | 1s pause at stretch | explosive drive | 1s lockout.

03
Horizontal Push
Incline Bench Press
4 × 6–8Tempo: 3-1-1-0Rest: 3m

Bar to upper chest (below collarbone). Control the descent strictly. 4 sets here — pairs with the forward-lean dips on Day A for complete chest coverage. Incline angle builds the upper chest shelf, dips handle lower/mid chest. 3s lowering bar to chest | 1s pause at chest | 1s pressing up | no pause at lockout.

04
Horizontal Pull
Seated Cable Rows
3 × 10–12Tempo: 2-0-1-1Rest: 2m

Sit tall, chest up, feet braced. Pull handle to lower ribcage, squeeze shoulder blades together. Don't lean back — torso stays upright throughout. 2s releasing cable forward | 1s pulling to ribcage | 1s squeeze at top.

05
Glutes
Barbell Hip Thrusts
3 × 8–10Tempo: 2-0-1-2Rest: 2m

Upper back on bench, feet flat, shin vertical at the top. Drive through heels, squeeze glutes hard at lockout — hold 2s. Don't hyperextend the lower back — think "ribs down, belt buckle to chin." The #1 glute builder and your running injury insurance. Protects knees, powers uphill running, builds the posterior shelf. 2s lowering hips | 1s driving up | 2s squeeze at lockout.

06
Calves
Seated Calf Raises
2 × 10–15Tempo: 2-2-1-2Rest: 90s

Knee bent at 90° loads the soleus — the wider, deeper calf muscle that sits underneath the gastrocnemius and pushes it out, adding overall calf width. Full stretch at bottom, full squeeze at top. Pause at both ends. 2s lowering heels | 2s pause at stretch | 1s pressing up | 2s squeeze at top.

Leg Swings: 10/side (front-to-back + side-to-side). Walking Lunges: 10 steps. High Knees: 20 steps. Ankle circles: 10/side. Don't static stretch cold muscles — save that for after.

02
The Session
Walk/Run Intervals
See weekly progression aboveSaturday = "long" session (slightly longer)

Walk briskly during walk phases — don't stroll. Run at a conversational pace during run phases — you should be able to speak in full sentences. If you can't talk, you're going too fast. Stay tall — don't hunch forward. Land midfoot, not on your heels. The walk/run ratio progresses each week — same easy effort, just more time running. Saturday is always the longest session to build endurance gradually. If a week feels hard, repeat it instead of progressing — consistency beats speed.

03
Post-Run
Cooldown Stretches
~5 minTempo: Static Holds, 30s each

Standing Quad Stretch: 30s/side. Standing Calf Stretch (straight leg for gastroc, bent knee for soleus): 30s each/side. Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch: 30s/side — same as your APT correction stretch, doubles as recovery. Hamstring doorway stretch or standing toe touch: 30s.

Phase 1 — Build the Base (You Are Here · Block 1 of ~3)

1.

Block 1 (now): Walk 1:30 / Run 1:00 → progressing to Walk 1:00 / Run 1:30 by Week 3. Deload Week 4.

2.

Block 2 (next): Walk 0:45 / Run 2:00 → Walk 0:30 / Run 2:30. Introduce Saturday as a dedicated longer session.

3.

Block 3: Walk 0:30 / Run 3:00 → continuous running. Saturday long run reaches 30+ min nonstop.

4.

Phase 1 complete when: You can run 30 min continuously at a conversational pace with no walk breaks.

Phase 2 — Build Mileage (3 Runs/Week)

5.

Structure: Tue = easy run (30 min) — Thu = easy run (30 min) — Sat = long run (start at 40 min, add 5 min every 2 weeks)

6.

All runs are easy: Zone 2, conversational pace. The long run is longer, not faster. Speed comes later — aerobic base comes first.

7.

10% rule: Never increase total weekly running time by more than 10% per week. Tendons and bones adapt slower than your lungs.

8.

Phase 2 complete when: Your long run reaches 75–80 min continuously (~8–9 km at easy pace)

Phase 3 — Half Marathon Ready

9.

Structure: Tue = easy run (30–40 min) — Thu = easy run (30–40 min) — Sat = long run (build to 100–110 min)

10.

Peak long run: Once you can run 100–110 min continuously (roughly 16–18 km at easy pace), you are half marathon ready. You don't need to run 21 km in training.

11.

Race week: Cut all running volume in half the week before race day. Lift normally. Rest Friday and Saturday. Race Sunday.

Block 1 — Lifting Protocol (Weeks 1–4)

1.

Weeks 1–3 — Progressive Overload: When you hit the top of the rep range on ALL sets of an exercise, add weight next session (2.5 kg upper, 5 kg lower). If you miss reps, stay at the same weight.

2.

Week 4 — Deload: Same exercises, same weight. Cut sets in half — 4→2, 3→2, 2→1. Keep intensity, slash volume. You will come back stronger. Run deload matches (see Run tab).

3.

Priority This Block: Hypertrophy is the #1 goal. Running is low-volume base building and should NOT compromise your lifting. If a run makes you too sore to lift, shorten or walk the next run.

The Hybrid Athlete Rules

1.

Lift Heavy, Lift Fast: Heavy straight sets, 3–8 rep range on compounds. Chase strength per unit of fatigue, not volume.

2.

The V-Taper: Built by lats & delts, revealed by leanness. Pulls equal pushes. Warm-up laterals every session.

3.

Run Easy: All running is zone 2 — conversational pace. The aerobic base reveals the physique and builds the engine. Never race your easy runs.

4.

Recovery is Training: Sunday is non-negotiable rest. Sleep 8–9+ hours — concurrent training demands more recovery. If legs are still sore on a run day, walk the full session instead — never skip, just scale down.

5.

Fuel the Machine: Protein 1.8–2.2 g/kg/day across 4+ meals. Don't skip carbs — glycogen depletion worsens the interference effect. Eat at maintenance or a slight surplus.

6.

Tempo Key: Eccentric – Pause Bottom – Concentric – Pause Top (e.g., 3-1-1-0). X = Explosive.
